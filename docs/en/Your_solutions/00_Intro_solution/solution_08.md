## 8. Definite Integrals
**Problem:** Calculate the area under the curve of the function $f(x) = \sin(x)$ from $x = 0$ to $x = \pi$.

* **Step 1 (Finding the Antiderivative):** To calculate the area under a curve, we need to find the definite integral. The indefinite integral of $\sin(x)$ is:
  $\int \sin(x) dx = -\cos(x) + C$

* **Step 2 (Applying Fundamental Theorem of Calculus):** We evaluate the antiderivative at the upper limit ($\pi$) and subtract the value at the lower limit ($0$):
  Area $= [-\cos(x)]_0^\pi$

* **Step 3 (Substitution and Calculation):**
  Area $= (-\cos(\pi)) - (-\cos(0))$
  Since $\cos(\pi) = -1$ and $\cos(0) = 1$:
  Area $= (-(-1)) - (-1) = 1 + 1 = 2$

* **Conclusion:** The total area under the sine curve from $0$ to $\pi$ is exactly **2 square units**.
